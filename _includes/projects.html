3. Racket Dragon

I've done this project about four times in at least two languages. Basically what I did was make a program to calculate and draw iterations of the dragon curve, a badass fractal rivaled only by the badassery of its namesake. I coded it very badly in Python, slightly less terribly in Python, acceptably in Python, and excellently in Racket if I do say so myself. The functional language was made for this stuff.

I met the dragon curve in Jurassic Park. The book. My favorite example of the dragon curve is the pattern formed if you fold a piece of paper back on itself as many times as possible and then let every fold open to a right angle.
